Letters Should Be Local, Signed, and Clear

The opinion page will make space for reader arguments without losing accountability.

By Opinion Editor

Coverage area
Tysons
Section
Opinion
Published
June 26, 2026
Topics
opinion, letters, policy

Article Text

A local opinion page works best when readers know the rules. Letters should be signed, focused on Tysons-area issues, and written in a way that helps neighbors understand the argument.

The Times will separate reported news from opinion and make labels obvious. Editorials, columns, guest essays, and letters should not borrow the authority of straight reporting.

The format is designed for short pieces, longer guest arguments, editor's notes, and response packages when a public issue generates several thoughtful submissions.

Strong opinion pages are not loud by default. They are clear, accountable, and close to home.
