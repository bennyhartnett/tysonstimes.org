*Sample community brief: The market, vendors, and reporting details below are fictional and provided to demonstrate the publishing system.*

For three morning hours, the fictional plaza feels smaller than it does during the workweek. Produce crates narrow the walking routes, neighbors stop to talk, and shoppers compare what can survive the trip home on a bus or bicycle.

The market brief format puts service information first. A real listing would verify operating dates, rain policy, parking and transit access, accepted payment methods, and whether nutrition benefits can be used. A caption records what was available on the photographed date rather than implying that every vendor attends every week.

## Before you go

- Confirm the date and weather policy with the organizer.
- Bring a reusable bag and ask vendors how they accept payment.
- Leave the marked pedestrian route clear when the plaza is crowded.

The article can later grow into a vendor profile, a seasonal guide, or a story about how public plazas are managed. Its short length is intentional: the important details remain easy to find on a phone while a reader is making weekend plans.
